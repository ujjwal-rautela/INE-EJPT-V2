Your Journey to eJPT v2 Certification Starts Here

Welcome to your personalized, all-in-one guide for conquering the eJPTv2 (eLearnSecurity Junior Penetration Tester) certification. This roadmap is designed for beginners, providing a clear, step-by-step plan to build your skills from the ground up. The eJPT is a 100% practical, 48-hour exam that proves you have the real-world skills to be a penetration tester. It covers the full pentesting workflow across four key domains:

Assessment Methodologies (25%)

Reconnaissance, scanning, and vulnerability identification.

Host & Network Auditing (25%)

Post-exploitation, finding sensitive data, and enumerating compromised systems.

Host & Network Pentesting (35%)

Exploitation with Metasploit, password attacks, and pivoting.

Web App Pentesting (15%)

Web enumeration and exploiting common web vulnerabilities.

This guide breaks down the complex syllabus into a manageable 4-week schedule. Use the navigation to explore prerequisites, the daily study plan, budget estimates, and helpful resources.

Prerequisites Checklist

Before you dive into the main study plan, ensure you have a solid grasp of these fundamentals. The official INE course covers these topics, but knowing them beforehand will make your journey much smoother and faster. This section helps you self-assess your readiness.

You must understand how computers talk to each other. Knowing the OSI model, TCP/IP, and common ports (like 80 for web traffic) is essential for identifying and exploiting services.

You will live in the terminal during the exam. Being comfortable with Linux (bash) and Windows (cmd) for navigating files and running tools is non-negotiable.

A general knowledge of how operating systems, web applications, and databases work provides the context for your attacks. You can't break what you don't understand.

Penetration testing is detective work. It requires curiosity, persistence, and the ability to troubleshoot when things don't work as expected.

Exam Day Strategy

Preparation is half the battle; execution is the other half. How you manage your 48 hours is critical. Do not rush into scanning. A methodical approach will save you time and prevent frustration.

Before you run a single command, read the Letter of Engagement and **every single exam question**. Group the questions by target IP in your notes. This creates a "loot list" and tells you exactly what you need to find, turning the questions into your guide.

The rhythm of the exam is: **Enumerate -> Exploit -> Enumerate Again**. After you get a foothold on a machine, your first step should be to enumerate it for more information (users, passwords, network connections) that could lead to the next target.

The eJPT is straightforward. If an exploit is not working after a reasonable amount of time, it's probably not the intended path. Document your attempt, note why it failed, and move on. Don't waste hours on a single vector.

This is a 48-hour marathon. Schedule breaks to clear your head. Remember, the exam includes a free retake, which is a strategic safety net. Don't panic if you get stuck.
